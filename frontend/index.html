SmartStreet Scientific Methodology

✕

SmartStreet combines graph theory, transport-demand modelling and standardized environmental physics into explainable street-design indices. Full equations, parameters and citations: docs/METHODOLOGY.md.

1. Network & Congestion

  • Centrality: Betweenness (Freeman 1977; Brandes 2001 algorithm, sampled pivots) and closeness on travel-time weights.
  • Modeled flow: relative betweenness × HCM-informed class capacity — a standard flow proxy where OD counts are unavailable (Kazerani & Winter 2009; Puzis et al. 2013).
  • Congestion: BPR volume-delay t = t₀(1 + 0.15·(v/c)⁴) (Bureau of Public Roads 1964) yields v/c and congested speeds, mappable as a layer.

2. Environmental Models

  • CO₂: COPERT / EMEP-EEA average-speed emission curves for petrol & diesel passenger cars (fleet-blended), evaluated at congested speed (Ntziachristos & Samaras 2019).
  • Noise: simplified CNOSSOS-EU source model (Directive (EU) 2015/996): rolling + propulsion sound power, traffic line source, receiver level at 10 m. WHO 2018 thresholds frame the "noisy km" indicator.

3. Active Travel

  • Walkability: infrastructure (0.35) + safety (0.20) + destinations within 250 m (0.25) + intersection density (0.20), per the built-environment "5 D" evidence (Ewing & Cervero 2010; Frank et al. 2010).
  • Cycling LTS 1–4: Mekuria, Furth & Nixon (2012) criteria using street speed/lanes joined via shared OSM way id.

4. StreetIQ Composite

Percentile-normalised weighted composite of centrality, flow, CO₂ and noise (user weights) — a burden/priority index marking streets that carry the network and concentrate externalities (OECD/JRC 2008 composite-indicator practice).

5. Multi-Year Agent Simulation

A mesoscopic land-use/transport interaction loop per simulated year (Wegener 2004; Ortúzar & Willumsen 2011):

  • Growth & location choice: exponential population growth allocated to zones by logit on accessibility + floor-space capacity (Hansen 1959; McFadden 1974; Waddell 2002).
  • Demand: gravity distribution with exponential impedance (Wilson 1971); multinomial-logit mode choice among car/transit/bike/walk with policy-driven constants (Train 2009).
  • Assignment: Method of Successive Averages over BPR-costed shortest paths — approximate Wardrop (1952) user equilibrium (Sheffi 1985).
  • Network evolution: budgeted densification & exploration rules — capacity upgrades where v/c > 1, new links where spatial detours are severe, optional pedestrianization of calm POI-rich streets (Strano et al. 2012; Barthélemy & Flammini 2008; Mueller et al. 2020).
  • Outputs: yearly mode shares, VKT/VHT, CO₂ t/day, noisy km, congested km, Hansen accessibility, network growth, investment — plus an animated sample of ~800 agents' space-time trajectories (visualization in the spirit of MATSim; Horni et al. 2016).

6. Optimization Suite

  • Signal placement: conflict exposure = betweenness × converging flow (cf. FHWA 2013).
  • Connectivity gaps: network/Euclidean detour-ratio screening (Dill 2004).
  • Direction solver: greedy one-way switches scored on travel time and CO₂.

7. Assumptions & Limitations

Flows are proxies, not counts; noise omits barriers/meteorology; simulation defaults require local calibration — results are scenario explorations, not forecasts; OSM completeness bounds accuracy (see the data-quality layer).
