SmartStreet Scientific Methodology

✕

SmartStreet integrates graph theory, traffic micro-simulation models, and environmental physics to build actionable, explainable indices for street design.

1. StreetIQ Composite Index

The StreetIQ index models the harmony between vehicular throughput utility and human-scale livability. It aggregates four key normalized indicators:

  • Centrality: Combines Betweenness Centrality (likelihood of a road being chosen as shortest path) and Closeness Centrality (how accessible a road is from all other points in the network) using NetworkX topological calculations.
  • Modeled Flow: Employs a gravity routing algorithm estimating neighborhood-level commuter traffic potential.
  • CO₂ Emissions: Approximated greenhouse impact based on simulated vehicular speeds, flow volume, and road-tier classification.
  • Noise Level (dB): A logarithmic acoustic simulation calculating decibel propagation based on traffic flow density and speed variables.

2. Active Travel Metrics

Indices representing walking and cycling comfort levels parsed from raw OSM geometry attributes:

  • Walkability Score: Evaluates pedestrian safety using pavement surface tags, pathway isolation, and nocturnal lighting.
  • Level of Traffic Stress (LTS): Ranks street segments (LTS 1 to 4) representing cycling feasibility, from quiet residential streets up to speed-mixed heavy traffic arterials.

3. Algorithmic Optimization Suite

Urban optimization functions designed to identify design enhancements:

  • Signal Placement: Identifies collision risk and pedestrian isolation conflict points at major road junctions using convergent capacity models.
  • Connectivity Gaps: Evaluates fragmented pathways to locate target locations where minor pedestrian/cycleway inserts yield maximum network integration.
  • Direction Solver: Performs greedy directionality switches across selected segments to minimize aggregate traffic bottlenecks and local emissions.
